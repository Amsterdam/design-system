The main colours are red, blue, black, and white.
They are complemented by two greys and seven supplemental colours.

## Brand tokens

The term 'Brand' represents the highest level in the three-tier token structure of the [NL Design System](https://nldesignsystem.nl/handboek/design-tokens/).

Our brand colour tokens have meaningful (semantic) names instead of generic labels like 'blue-60' or 'red-100' that other design systems may use.
This approach ensures that the correct colour is used in the appropriate context.
Always use a token for its intended purpose based on its name.
Please reach out to us if you require a different colour token.

## Pairing foreground with background colours

### White background

Most of the background of our pages is white.
All text is black here, and all kinds of links are blue.
Obviously, these colours are not always effective on other backgrounds.

- Text: `var(--ams-color-text)`
- Links: `var(--ams-color-interactive)`

### Light background

We consider <InlineColorSample color="lime" /> **lime**, <InlineColorSample color="orange" /> **orange** and <InlineColorSample color="yellow" /> **yellow** to be ‘light’ backgrounds.
Black text still provides enough contrast here.
Links on these backgrounds are black as well – this is the **‘contrast’** interactive colour.
The underline and cursor still distinguish links from static text.

- Text: `var(--ams-color-text)`
- Links: `var(--ams-color-interactive-contrast)`

### Dark background

An <InlineColorSample color="azure" /> **azure**, <InlineColorSample color="blue" /> **blue**, <InlineColorSample color="green" /> **green**, <InlineColorSample color="magenta" /> **magenta**, <InlineColorSample color="purple" /> **purple** or <InlineColorSample color="red" /> **red** background is ‘dark’.
On these, both text and links are white – the **‘inverse’** text and interactive colour.

- Text: `var(--ams-color-text-inverse)`
- Links: `var(--ams-color-interactive-inverse)`
